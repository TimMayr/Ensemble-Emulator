**iNES Mapper 079** denotes the 72-pin **NINA-03** and **NINA-06** circuit boards, produced by [American Video Entertainment](http://community.fandom.com/wiki/c:bootleggames:American_Video_Entertainment "wikia:c:bootleggames:American Video Entertainment"). They only differ in the nature of the CIC â€” the former always has a microcontroller, the latter may instead have a charge pump. The [iNES](INES.xhtml "INES") format designates **mapper 79** to NINA-03 and NINA-06. 

**iNES Mapper 146** denotes the 60-pin **Sachen 3015** circuit board, which is functionally identical.

## Banks

  * CPU $8000-$FFFF: 32 KB switchable PRG ROM bank
  * PPU $0000-$1FFF: 8 KB switchable CHR ROM bank



## Registers

### Control Register

This register is located at addresses: 
    
    
    F        bit      0
    ---- ---- ---- ----
    010x xxx1 xxxx xxxx
    

That is, $4100-$41FF, $4300-$43FF, $45xx, $47xx, ..., $5Dxx, and $5Fxx. 
    
    
    7  bit  0
    ---- ----
    .... PCCC
         ||||
         |+++-- Select 8 KB CHR ROM bank for PPU $0000-$1FFF
         +----- Select 32 KB PRG ROM bank for CPU $8000-$FFFF
    

## Variants

HES/AVE released three multicarts of NINA-06 games (_HES 6-in-1_ , _Mind Blower Pak_ , and _Total Funpak (HES)_), which is [mapper 113](INES_Mapper_113.xhtml "INES Mapper 113"). The multicart increases the bankable memory to 256 KiB PRG and 128 KiB CHR, and adds run-time selectable mirroring. [iNES Mapper 148](INES_Mapper_148.xhtml "INES Mapper 148") has the same bit assignment in the data latch, but moves the latch register to the CPU $8000-$FFFF register, introducing bus conflicts. 

## Errata

For mapper 79 but not 146, FCEUX additionally implements a CNROM switch at CPU $8000-$FFFF, apparently to run a few [mapper 3](CNROM.xhtml "INES Mapper 003") games that are incorrectly set to mapper 79 in GoodNES 3.23b.
