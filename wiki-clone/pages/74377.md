# 74377

From [NESdev Wiki](Nesdev_Wiki.xhtml) | View [current version](https://www.nesdev.org/wiki/74377) | View [other pages](Special_AllPages.xhtml#74377)

The 74377 chip (common variants 74LS377, 74HC377) is an octal D flip-flop, used as a latch in mappers like [Color Dreams](Color_Dreams.xhtml "Color Dreams") and stores eight bits of data. 

If only four or less bits are needed, a [74161](74161.xhtml "74161") may be used instead. 

See also: [74161 Latch Implementation](74161.xhtml#Latch_Implementation "74161")

## 74377 Pinout
    
    
         .--\/--.
    /WE -| 1  20|- Vcc
     Q0 -| 2  19|- Q7
     D0 -| 3  18|- D7
     D1 -| 4  17|- D6
     Q1 -| 5  16|- Q6
     Q2 -| 6  15|- Q5
     D2 -| 7  14|- D5
     D3 -| 8  13|- D4
     Q3 -| 9  12|- Q4
    GND -|10  11|- CLK
         `------'
    

## Signal descriptions

  * Vcc: Connect to +5V
  * GND:
  * CLK: Load clock
  * /WE: Write enable
  * D0-7: Inputs, connected to CPU D0-D7
  * Q0-7: Outputs, connected to PRG and CHR ROM address lines



D0-7 are copied to Q0-7 when CLK rises (0 to 1) while /WE is low.
